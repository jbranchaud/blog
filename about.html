About me

I do research

I am a graduate research assistant studying computer science at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln working in the area of software engineering. My research involves software development coordination and awareness issues as well as program analysis. Yeah, I know, those things don't really seem all that related, but bridging the gap has been interesting. Here are my publications so far.

I write code

Writing code is fun which is probably why I do a lot of it in my free time. I have a variety of side projects that I host on GitHub. I grew up in Java land (which is where I am most proficient), but I am enamored with languages like python and ruby. In fact, I have been using python for most of my side projects lately.

Jealousy of Omaha's Beer && Code meetup led Branden and myself to form a similar meetup in Lincoln which we have aptly named Beer and Code. It is a great opportunity for Lincoln-area developers to socialize, write code, and drink good beer.

I shoot photos

Photography has always been a passion of mine. Moving up from a simple point-and-shoot to a DSLR (Nikon D3100) has only encouraged the hobby. I host most of my photos on Flickr, but you can find me on 500px as well.

I shoot pool

Though there was a pool table in my house for a number of years growing up, it wasn't until the last couple years that I really started to take the game seriously. I have now hosted three 8 ball leagues at the Lutheran Center and I also played in Lincoln's 2012-2013 Great Plains city pool league.
